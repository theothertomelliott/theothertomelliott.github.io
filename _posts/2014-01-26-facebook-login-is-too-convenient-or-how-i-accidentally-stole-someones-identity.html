More and more websites and services are making use of Facebook to save users the chore of creating a new account. A great time-saver, maybe, but with convenience comes a greater chance to cock things up royally.

"Hey, can I use your laptop to check my Facebook?" Surprisingly dangerous words. Not just because your friend might post suggestive messages on your feed.

Friend still logged in on my phone, redeemed a LivingSocial voucher. Saw her name come up.

Would be possible to try a number of well known sites to access their accounts.

Shopping sites would be susceptible.

Could mess with their social life.

Destroy their farms.

This makes Facebook the weak link. They recently moved to all https to prevent easy theft of login tokens from Wi-Fi.
